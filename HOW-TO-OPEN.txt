KeepAwake ☕  —  how to open it the first time
================================================

This app was built by Abhinav, not downloaded from the App Store, so macOS will
ask you to approve it once. This is normal and safe. After the first time it just
opens like any other app.

FIRST LAUNCH (macOS Sonoma / Sequoia / 26)
-------------------------------------------
1. Drag KeepAwake.app into your Applications folder (optional, but tidy).
2. Double-click KeepAwake.app.
3. A box says something like "Apple could not verify KeepAwake is free of
   malware."  Click  Done  (do NOT click Move to Trash).
4. Open  System Settings  ->  Privacy & Security.
5. Scroll down to the  Security  section. You'll see:
       "KeepAwake was blocked to protect your Mac."
   Click the  Open Anyway  button next to it.
6. Authenticate with Touch ID or your password, then click  Open Anyway  again.
7. Done. A coffee-cup icon appears in your menu bar (top-right). Click it to use.

(On older macOS: right-click KeepAwake.app -> Open -> Open.)

Note: the signer shows as "abhinav". Because it isn't a paid Apple Developer
certificate, macOS still calls it "unidentified" — the Open Anyway step above is
how you approve it. Nothing to worry about.

WHAT IT DOES
------------
Click the cup, pick how long to stay awake (15 min / 30 min / 1 hr / Indefinite /
Custom). Keep the "Keep running with lid closed" box checked to let the Mac keep
running with the lid shut (great for downloads/builds). Hit Stop to return to
normal — it never leaves your Mac unable to sleep.

OPTIONAL EXTRAS (power users)
-----------------------------
Open Terminal, cd into this folder, then:
  bash install.sh                 # auto-start at login
  sudo bash grant-admin.sh        # lid-closed mode with NO password prompt
  sudo bash enable-touchid-sudo.sh# Touch ID for sudo

CAUTIONS
--------
* Running with the lid closed traps heat — fine for light/medium work, be careful
  with heavy sustained CPU/GPU load.
* On battery, a closed-lid session runs until the battery dies. Keep it plugged in.
* On a managed (Jamf) work Mac, the sudo step may be blocked or reverted by IT.
